# Writing a good case study

Fill these in the Studio (https://mohameddesign.sanity.studio) per project. For a
brand-identity portfolio, the **images do ~80% of the work — the words just frame them.**
Keep text tight and let the work carry it.

## The shape

**Hero image** — your single best frame. Not the logo on white — the logo *living*
somewhere (a sign, a package, a flagship application), or a bold full-bleed of the mark.
This is the thumbnail and first impression; pick the one frame that makes someone stop.

**Intro (100–150 words)** — three beats, no filler:
1. **Who + the challenge** — one line on the client and what they needed.
2. **The idea** — the single concept the identity is built on. This sentence shows you
   *think*, not just decorate. It's what separates a senior case study from a generic one.
3. **What you delivered** — scope in plain words (logo, type, colour, packaging, …).

Cut anything that could appear on *any* project ("a modern, clean identity" → delete).
Specific beats impressive.

**Application images (gallery)** — sequence like a story, ~6–12 images:
1. The mark, big and clean.
2. Construction / grid or the logic behind it.
3. Type + colour (one board each).
4. Real applications — packaging, signage, cards, social, merch (the more it looks
   *in the world*, the more it sells).
5. A detail shot or two.

Consistency is everything: same background tone, same lighting, no random drop shadows.
One inconsistent mockup cheapens the whole set.

**Outcome (1–2 lines)** — close with the result. Metrics if you have them; otherwise a
confident qualitative line. Don't overclaim — quiet confidence reads as senior.

## Per-project checklist
- [ ] Hero = your single strongest frame
- [ ] Intro names the *idea*, not just adjectives
- [ ] Sector / services / year filled (they're the meta line)
- [ ] 6–12 application images, consistent backgrounds, ordered mark → system → applications → details
- [ ] Outcome line that's true, not inflated
- [ ] Every image earns its place — 6 great beats 12 okay

## One trap to avoid
Don't make every project a full epic. Pick the **3–4 strongest** for deep case studies
(rich intro + full gallery) and let the rest stand as a strong hero + meta. A few deep,
many tight reads as more curated than everything being medium.
